What is an Aging Life Care Manager?

A caregiver provides care. A care manager makes sure you receive the right care, from the right people, at the right time.

Care management is not caregiving.

You may be familiar with caregiving — the hands-on help a family member or hired caregiver provides: meals, hygiene, transportation, companionship.

Care management is something different. Our Aging Life Care Professionals® — nurses, social workers, and health specialists — assess your full situation, build a personalized plan, and coordinate every moving part of your loved one's care.

Simply put: a caregiver provides care. A Care Manager makes sure you receive the right care, from the right people, at the right time.

Caregiver

Provides the care

Hands-on daily help: meals, hygiene, transportation, companionship.

Care Manager

Coordinates the care

Assesses needs, builds the plan, attends appointments, advocates across the system.

Why Families Call Us

5 reasons to bring in a Care Manager

01

Expertise you don't have

Our team holds nursing, social work, and dementia certifications — and decades of experience navigating the aging-care system.

02

One trusted point of contact

Instead of juggling doctors, home-health agencies, and facilities yourself, you have one professional coordinating it all.

03

Continuity of care

We stay involved as your loved one's needs change — through transitions, hospital stays, and new diagnoses.

04

Cost containment

The right care, at the right level, in the right setting — saving families from costly over- or under-care.

05

Peace of mind

Especially for long-distance families: a knowledgeable advocate on the ground who calls, visits, and reports back.

What We Do

Nine areas of life we help families navigate

Housing

Evaluate aging-in-place, residential care, assisted living, memory care.

Home Care Services

Source, screen, and supervise in-home caregivers when needed.

Medical Management

Attend appointments, manage medications, coordinate specialists.

Communication

Keep family members informed and aligned, wherever they live.

Social Activities

Connect clients to programs and communities that keep them engaged.

Legal Navigation

Coordinate with elder-law attorneys for advance directives, POAs, and more.

Financial Coordination

Work alongside financial advisors and family to plan for care costs.

Entitlements

Help navigate Medicare, Medicaid, VA benefits, and long-term care insurance.

Safety & Security

Home assessments, fall prevention, technology solutions for aging in place.
